Overview

Our project is to develop a website that will enable users to find a wide variety of charity works, conservation efforts, community projects or other crowd funded projects across Australia. These charities and projects will be displayed on our site after they have been identified as an honest and impactful charity through an analysis conducted by our team. By doing this, people can open our site, browse across a country map, view a wide variety of charity works, knowing that all these charities are going to put their donations to good use and be used for the purpose that they have detailed.
The best part of the app is that it will provide the donor will all the information they need to pick the most suitable charity for them. The app will show the user all the relevant information about a selected charity. It will show them exactly where their money is going and how the charity uses that money. For example; how much money actually goes to those in need and how much money goes into the charity for things like advertising. This will give the donor piece of mind. Also, if this app is successful, it will increase the amount of money that goes to needing charities. This is because people of the public will be able to make a better and more personal decision about where their money is going. They will also be more aware of charities that suit them and that need money.

Motivation

In Australia, there are over 56,000 registered charities. These range from education, health, religion, human rights, animal welfare, the environment and social welfare. There are many philanthropic people in Australia who want to donate to a specific charity but are either uneducated to which charity is best for them. Or they are so overwhelmed by the number of charities in Australia, that they don’t know where to start. There are some people that chose to donate to charity as it is a deductable expense on their income tax statement.
There is also currently a huge amount of distrust with charities across Australia. This is partially due to several large charities taking in millions of dollars of donations for the 2019/2020 summer bushfires relief and large portions of these donations were not being sent to though that needed immediate help. This is one of the reasons that this website will help. It will give donors an accurate indication of where their money is really going.
This is a very admirable and noble cause as it is based around registered charities that support the needy. If this project is successful, more people will have access to donate to charities that matter to them. Testing

We will be undertaking two different types of testing for Quality Assurance, Namely White and Black Box to make sure testing has some variation, during different Phases of our Project.

No testing needs to take place, within the first stage of the Project as it is mainly Surveying and Brainstorming on a User Interface Design.

During the Alpha stage within the First Few Weeks of the program, while we only have a prototype, We will run through Acceptance Testing, making sure all functions of the website work using a planned set of instructions to make sure all pre-defined functions work, Close Friends and Family members can assist with Black Box testing (testing without knowing the parameters of the of the software) and off course the group ourselves who will be white box testers. Ad-Hoc Testing is also Essential during this time as it is without reference and unplanned use of the software to make sure any unknown glitches and software/security breaches are found. Back End Testing to check if customer data and other website back end data is Functional; Can also be tested by the software Engineers. Since our Website will be used by a wide variety of customers Compatibility testing will be run by testing our Site on many different browsers and Devices.

Next is the Beta Phase of Testing, when we are happy with the first stages of testing the Alpha Prototype we can move to a wider audience of testers and complete most of the same tastes as in the Alpha Stage but far more rigorous. This will be the Final Stage of Testing and therefore stress Testing occurs just before release, this includes tasks such as seeing how many users our server can handle at once and inputting large amounts of data into the website at one time to test when and how it fails.

Final or Full release of our Website, will still need to have Testing Parameters met, as once the up and running there will still need to be Maintenance and updated features to the website which will need their own set of Alpha and Beta Testing also known as Unit Testing, which is completed by senior programmers, as they improve the site as it continues to function and requires knowledge of the internal programming code. Risks

Risk is inherent in any project undertaking, whether it is an IT project or a business project. It is vitally important that the team leader, manager, or developers can identify these risks before they happen. It is important that someone has the responsibility to identify the risks in the project and take the proper steps to mitigate them.
In the words of Duke Leto Atreides from the novel Dune “Knowing that a trap exists is the first step in avoiding it”.
This philosophy towards risk is vitally important when initiating an IT team project.
What exactly is risk?
Risk is defined as “a future uncertain event with a probability of occurrence and potential for loss.” (Jos, Jos and Anand, 2020)
Risks can take many forms in an IT project. There are internal and external risks. Schedule risks, budget risks, operational risks, scope risks, technical risks and programmatic risks.
These risks will apply as much to “Choice Charities” donation website as any other IT project.
As university students who have not had any experience so far in a professional IT team project, we must be vigilant in trying to identify the risks in the development of Choice Charities so that they can be mitigated, further increasing the probability of its success.

Risks in communication are biggest causes of failure in a team project. A team must work coherently and have open channels of communication. Each team member must know their place in the project so that there is a positive and productive culture.
With Choice Charities, we are planning to mitigate this risk by having group meetings twice a week and taking down documentation. Each member knows their place in the project and can be productive on their own. If for whatever reason one of the team members had to leave the project, there would not be a massive void. As a new member could quickly take on their responsibility or other members would be able to know exactly what needed to be done due to written documentation.

Understanding the scope and limits is one of the most important parts of an entire project. Everything must be planned in hindsight so that the project has as much chance of success as possible. The key stakeholders must be identified. The different roles of the team members have must be identified. Different iterations must be established. Also the budged must be established so that deadlines must be met.
For many IT projects these constraints are all well and good at the start of a project. But then something called scope creep happens.
Scope creep is where you start off project with a set of clear, well defined requirements, but as you approach the finish line so many requirements are added or deleted that all you are left with is one big mess. (4 Common Risks In Software Development Projects, 2020).
This is big a risk that could happen to the development of Choice Charities. It is possible that in the production of the website so many things are added and deleted that the developers do not know their roles anymore. The technical project manager may lose sight of the flow of the project. The senior web designer may be overwhelmed with all the programming he must do. The research analysist who is responsible for studying, evaluating, interpreting and presenting data may lose sight of their role in the project. This could cause chaos among the project and could quickly derail it.
To mitigate the risk of scope creep it would be necessary to have constant feedback from customer feedback and product owners.

Technical difficulties are another risk when it comes to an IT project. There are 2 types of technical difficulties: hardware and software. These technical risks would come about by improper design.
With Choice Charities, some examples of technical risk would be:
• Data security –refers to the process of protecting data from unauthorized access and data corruption throughout its lifecycle. Data security includes data encryption, hashing, tokenization, and key management practices that protect data across all applications and platforms. (What is Data Security? | Micro Focus, 2020)
• Information privacy
• Large scale system implementation
• Software integration
• Compliance

Some examples of software risk
There is always the chance that there are bugs in the code. This is the responsibly of the Full Stack Developer/Senior Web Designer and the Front End Web Developer/Lead Web designers. This risk is increased when there are problems with planning, so that the coders are rushed and don’t take necessary care with the code.
There could be other errors in the code so that when it is uploaded on the internet, the website crashes. To mitigate this risk there should be extensive quality control and beta testing.

Hardware risks stem from the website hosting service. It is possible that there is always some risk that the hosting service will run into unforeseen problems and the website will not be published on the internet.

Choice Charities will be a website where people can make donations to any charity of their choosing.
This means that there is a massive risk of security. When people are using their credit cards or PayPal to give donate. It is vitally important that the website is fully secure and encrypted so that the money is securely transferred to the charity. 100% of their money must go to the charity.
The website must be hacker proof. There are many seedy people out there who like to take advantage of the vulnerable and try and grift money away from them. This means that security is a massive risk with Choice Charities. Again, the Full Stack Developer/Senior Web Designer and Front End Web Developer/Lead Web designers must incorporate a high level of encryption and security within the project.
Another risk related to having charities is that every charity on the website must be 100% legitimate.
Each charity must be thoroughly researched before it is listed on the website. After all, this is the main purpose that the project has been incepted at its very beginning. Each charity must be an Official Registered Charity. Also there must be research into what percentage of donations are distributed by the charity. For example, what percentage goes to admin fees, or advertisement. This is highly important as it is the sole reason for this project existing in the first place.
The risk that all charities are legitimate should be taken up by the research analysist.



Poor quality code is a massive risk when it come to an IT project. Sometimes the project is underestimated, and developers rush to complete the iteration. The code can be difficult to read and difficult for other developers to review or make changes. (What are the 10 biggest risks in software development?, 2020)
The code can be rushed and released without testing and full of bugs that could have been prevented.
“Technical debt” is where developers take shortcuts when writing the code to achieve their goals faster. (What are the 10 biggest risks in software development?, 2020)
For Choice Charities technical debt is a big risk. The A Full Stack Developer/Senior Web Designer has the potential to cause errors in the code if they are not careful. This could be due to shifting constraints in budgeting and scope of the project. This also applies to the technical project manager and the research analysist.
When there is scope creep, this could cause the web designer to have to take shortcuts.
The code for Choice Charities must be written correctly. This is so that the website will have longevity. Code for choice charities must be easy to maintain, understand and extend. The code must be documented well so that if there are any new team members, they can quickly pick up where the previous coder left off.

Lack of ownership is a risk that can lead to the failure of Choice Charities.
With Choice Charities, lack of ownership is a risk that could lead to project failure. Someone must be accountable for each step in the project.
For whatever reason, the CEO does not take responsibly. This would have flow on effects for the whole project including the scope and budgeting.
To mitigate this risk, an experienced and diligent CEO would have to be hired.


Choice Charities will face many risks, but with proper planning and with internal controls in place, we will be able to identify risks before they are a problem and will be able to mitigate them so that our project is successful.
